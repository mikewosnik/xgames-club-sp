# I. Foundational Spirit & Club Identity

## What is XC São Paulo
XC São Paulo is one of the founding clubs of the X Games League (XGL). It is not a national team, not a flag, and not a brand exercise. It is a living expression of the culture of skateboarding and action sports as they exist in one of the world's great cities — grounded in progression, creativity, resilience, and shared growth.
The club exists to create a shared environment where athletes and disciplines stand on their own, where growth accelerates through connection, exchange, and an ongoing loop of inspiration across generations.

## General Manager — Bob Burnquist

Bob Burnquist is one of the most influential figures in the history of X Games, recognized for his longevity, creativity, and relentless pursuit of progression. A Brazilian American professional who turned pro as a teenager, Burnquist built his competitive career through both high level performance and a deep commitment to learning, experimentation, and evolution, blending results with innovation and imagination at the highest level.
Alongside competition, Burnquist has played a defining role in skateboarding's technical and creative development through landmark video parts and ongoing projects that continue to shape how the culture is practiced and perceived. His approach, from concepts like the Open Loop to switch progression and long form visual storytelling, reflects a belief that progression is not a single moment, but an ongoing process that connects performance, creativity, and personal growth.
Beyond skateboarding, Burnquist embodies a broader lifestyle rooted in movement, curiosity, and exploration. A licensed skydiver and pilot, he draws inspiration across disciplines and carries that same spirit of focus, courage, and presence into everything he does. He leads Instituto Skate Cuida, a social initiative that uses skateboarding as a tool for education, inclusion, and positive change, and has long supported sustainability and responsible growth within skateboarding communities.
As General Manager of X Games Club São Paulo, Burnquist brings a lived understanding of competition, crew culture, and shared progression. His focus is on building a team grounded in humility, creativity, and mutual support, honoring the raw roots of skateboarding while creating space for performance, evolution, and long term growth.

"I was raised in São Paulo, and that environment shaped how I skateboard and how I move through the world. The streets, nature, the people, the work ethic — all of it stays with me. This culture has never been about where you are from. It is about who you are, how you show up, how you inspire others, and what you bring to the session. That is the spirit I am building around X Games Club São Paulo."
— Bob Burnquist, General Manager, XC São Paulo

# II. X Games League — Structure & Mechanics

## What is the X Games League
The X Games League (XGL) is a global, team-based professional sports league layered onto the traditional X Games. Athletes are drafted to city-based clubs and earn points for their team across a season while still competing as individuals. Traditional X Games events continue alongside the League structure.

## How the Draft Works
The draft is a snake-format selection across 5 rounds among 4 founding clubs. Draft order is determined by lottery. Each round requires one male and one female athlete — 10 total athletes per club (5 men, 5 women). There is no requirement to draft athletes from any specific discipline.
Athletes drafted in Rounds 1 and 2 choose a 2-year or 3-year contract term at their option. Athletes drafted in Rounds 3 through 5 receive a 3-year term.

## XC São Paulo Draft Position — 2026
XC São Paulo drew the 4th lottery position, selecting last in Round 1 and first in Round 4. SP's exact pick numbers: #4, #5 (R1), #11, #14 (R2), #18, #23 (R3), #25, #32 (R4), #36, #37 (R5).


## How the Championship Works
Every point an athlete earns in their individual discipline counts toward their Club's total. The Club with the highest combined points at the end of the season wins the Club Championship and a Championship Bonus of $500,000 USD shared among the winning team's athletes, pro-rated by competition participation. Athletes still compete individually and earn discipline medals and performance bonuses at each stop.

## Scoring System
Placements in each discipline earn points. The system is heavily top-weighted — first place earns significantly more than mid-field finishes. Performance bonuses are awarded at each stop based on final discipline placement across two pay scales at the League's discretion.

## Athlete Obligations
All drafted athletes compete at every stop unless on the Injury Reserve List or approved for a Bona Fide Withdrawal. Athletes in Rounds 1–2: 5 annual promotional appearances (4 social posts + 1 on-site club appearance). Athletes in Rounds 3–5: 7 annual appearances (5 social posts + 2 club appearances). Each stop, the GM submits 2 athletes for league-run media. Non-compliance can result in a 20-point Club deduction.

## Club Outfitting
Athletes wear the official Club shirt during competition, podium, and league/club media. Athletes with personal apparel partners may place their sponsor's logo patch on the sleeve (preferred option) or wear an apparel partner shirt in club colors with team crest visible. Helmets and personal equipment remain open to personal sponsors.

## Travel & Compensation
The League coordinates all travel and lodging for athletes at each stop. Athletes should not book independent travel. All drafted athletes receive a standardized contractor service fee paid in monthly installments across the four-month season. They are independent contractors responsible for their own taxes.
